Mahendra Singh Dhoni, born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand, India, is a former Indian cricketer and one of the most iconic figures in the sport. Dhoni was renowned for his exceptional wicket-keeping skills, aggressive batting, and astute captaincy. He captained the Indian cricket team from 2007 to 2016, leading India to victories in the 2007 ICC World Twenty20, the 2010 and 2016 Asia Cups, the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy. Known as "Captain Cool," Dhoni was praised for his calm demeanor under pressure. He retired from international cricket in July 2020, leaving an indelible mark on the game.

T20 WORLD CUP (2007)

In the 2007 T20 World Cup, India displayed exceptional cricketing prowess, led by captain Mahendra Singh Dhoni. They triumphed against all odds, winning the inaugural tournament and etching their names in cricket history.

ODI WORLD CUP (2011)

The 2011 Cricket ODI World Cup captivated fans worldwide with its intense matches, incredible batting performances, and India's memorable triumph, ending a 28-year wait for the coveted title.

CHAMPIONS TROPHY (2013)

In the 2013 Champions Trophy, India showcased their dominance in limited-overs cricket. Led by captain MS Dhoni, they clinched the title with a flawless performance, displaying exceptional skills and teamwork throughout the tournament.
